Foundation

Horizon Consumer Product Vision 🔒 Version locked

Horizon guides each consumer through the experience they need, based on their current financial situation and the choices they've already made—without unnecessary friction.

🔗
An End‑to‑End Consumer Payment Experience
Horizon provides one continuous experience—from deciding how to pay when amounts are still estimated to completing payment once balances are final—so consumers always see the next action, understand why it matters, and can move seamlessly from planning to payment.
🎯
Guided and Personalized, Not Generic
The experience adapts to each user's situation, decisions, account changes, and constraints—showing what matters to them, not every possible option at once.
💡
Decision‑First Experiences
Don't just tell users what they owe, when it's due, and how to pay.
Horizon presents what they need to decide on, based on their situation, and clearly explains why that decision matters.
📊
Relevance Without Overload
Empower users with timely, relevant information—starting with what they need to know, and providing seamless access to full details when deeper understanding is required.
💬
Changes Communicated in Natural Language
Users don't understand change through labels—they understand it through reasons and outcomes.
Horizon communicates updates using a clear cause → effect model, explaining what triggered the change and what it means for the user.
⚡
Fewer Clicks, Fewer Screens
Consumers come here to pay for school.
Horizon removes unnecessary steps and screens so users can complete what they came to do—quickly and confidently.
🚫
No Duplicate Entry. Ever.
Students are never asked to re‑enter information the school already has.
Horizon pulls data directly from the institution's ERP and uses existing Nelnet payment history term over term—confirming or adjusting only what's changed.
💳
Modern Payment Experience
Horizon looks and feels like the modern payment systems consumers use every day.
If I want to pay with Apple Pay or other modern methods, I can—without workarounds or barriers.
📱
Phone & Text First
Meet users where they already are.
Text and phone are primary—not secondary—channels for communication and support.
🏁
Outcome
Horizon removes unnecessary screens, steps, and assumptions—replacing them with guided, role‑aware experiences that reflect how people actually manage money today.
Whether I'm responsible for paying, learning how to pay, or making sure someone else does, Horizon shows me exactly what matters now—and what to do next.

Onboarding Vision 🔒 Version locked

Set-it-and-forget-it outcome. Once onboarding is complete, a student who makes no changes to their account or payment method should realistically never need to re-enter the system during the term if they don't want to. All required decisions, preferences, and payment setup must be fully captured upfront.

One decision at a time. Onboarding is structured around presenting one decision at a time. Each question reveals the next only after the previous is answered. If a later decision would logically prompt reconsideration of an earlier answer, editing backward is always possible — but the student is never asked to handle two open decisions at once.

ERP-first data strategy. Any value the ERP already holds — name, student ID, charges, financial aid, enrollment status, contact info from SSO — must be pre-populated. Students are never asked to type what the system already knows. The experience confirms or adjusts existing data; it does not re-collect it.

Scenario-driven communication setup. Onboarding collects the specific information needed to personalize every subsequent communication and to-do for that student's exact situation: payment plan type, authorized parties added, insurance decisions, and communication preferences all feed into what the student sees and receives after signup.

Horizon Consumer Communications Vision 🔒 Owner authored

Horizon communications proactively guide consumers into the right next action using ERP‑aligned, role‑aware messaging that is clear, timely, and never overwhelming.

📡
ERP‑First Communications
Horizon treats the school ERP as the system of record for who should be contacted, what they owe, and what changed—so communications reflect the institution's truth, not whether someone has logged into Horizon yet.
🌐
Reach the Full Population, Not Just Portal Users
Institutions shouldn't have to rely on consumers logging in to get paid. Horizon proactively communicates to anyone with an amount due (based on ERP data) to drive awareness and action into the platform.
⚡
Event‑Driven and Proactive by Default
Nelnet owns the responsibility of proactive, event‑triggered communications—bringing consumers in to pay, prompting required actions, and notifying them as balances, aid, plans, payments, or fees change.
📦
Built for Schools: Ready‑to‑Run, Not Build‑Your‑Own
Horizon comes with Nelnet‑written communications designed for best‑practice outcomes, so schools don't have to do work to make communications effective.
🎛️
Institution Control Without Institutional Burden
Schools can disable Horizon's suggested messages if they already cover them—but nothing is required for communications to work out of the box.
🎨
School Branding and Verbiage Where It Matters
Schools can tailor communication voice without rebuilding templates—through controlled areas like subject line and a school‑specific message section, while required compliance content remains protected.
✅
Consent Honored, Rights Respected
Consumers and Authorized Parties can opt out where legally permitted, but shouldn't have to do extra work to opt into communications they already consented to through their institution.
🎯
Role‑Aware Messaging (Don't Spam the Wrong Person)
Horizon adjusts communication content based on the user's role identified during onboarding (payer vs non‑payer vs authorized party), so people only get messages that matter to what they're responsible for.
📊
Relevance Without Overload
Communications start with what the user needs to know now, with deeper detail available when needed—so we avoid message fatigue while still providing full transparency.
🔗
One Message When One Action Causes Multiple Updates
When a single action generates multiple related updates, Horizon consolidates them into one communication so the user gets the full story without getting blasted.
💬
Cause → Effect Clarity
Every message clearly states: what this is, what triggered it, and what it means—using natural language that explains reasons and outcomes, not internal labels.
🚀
Action‑First with Direct Links
If the consumer needs to do something, the communication makes it effortless by deep‑linking them directly into the exact Horizon step to complete the action.
⏰
Timing and Priority Are Always Explicit
Communications always clarify how urgent something is and when action is needed, so users can prioritize without logging in.
📱
Phone & Text First
Horizon treats SMS and phone as primary engagement channels (not afterthoughts), while honoring consent and compliance requirements tied to those channels.

Pay Over Time Consumer Vision 🔒 Owner authored

Horizon enables consumers to pay over time in a way that fits their real lives—flexible, transparent, and easy to adjust as circumstances change.

💵
Built Around How I Actually Get Paid
Horizon lets me choose a payment cadence that reflects how I receive income—weekly, bi‑weekly, monthly—so my tuition payments align naturally with my paycheck.
🧭
Guided One Decision at a Time
Pay‑over‑time setup is intentionally simple. Horizon asks me one meaningful question at a time, helping me make confident decisions without overwhelming me with math or configuration.
📅
First Payment Timing That Makes Sense
I can choose when my first payment occurs so it happens after I get paid—while clearly seeing the rules my school has set for allowable timeframes.
💡
Clear Up‑Front Cost Transparency
When I decide how much to pay today, Horizon clearly shows what that means—total due today including any fees or optional services—so there are no surprises.
🎚️
Flexible Down Payments, Flexible Futures
I can decide how much to pay upfront without locking myself into a rigid plan that doesn't reflect my financial reality.
🔄
Easy Adjustments as Life Changes
Horizon makes it simple to update my payment frequency or scheduled dates later—so if my work pay schedule changes, my tuition plan can change with it.
🤝
Grace When I Need It
If money is tight, I can easily adjust just my next payment amount or date—without having to break the entire plan or call for help.
⏩
Pay Ahead When I Want Peace of Mind
If I'd rather not worry about an upcoming due date or a potential auto‑withdrawal failure, Horizon makes it easy to pay my next installment early, on my terms.
👥
Shared Responsibility, My Way
Horizon lets me naturally split tuition costs between myself and others—even when we're paying over time—by percentage, fixed amount, or specific charges, without complexity or manual work.
📊
Predictable Handling of Balance Changes
When what I owe increases, Horizon respects my preference—either asking me how I want to handle it right away or automatically distributing it evenly across remaining payments so things stay predictable.
🆘
A Way to Ask for Help—Without Breaking the System
If I can't afford a certain monthly amount, Horizon gives me a clear way to signal that I may need approval to extend my plan—so I can pay less per month without falling behind.
🏁
Outcome
Paying over time with Horizon feels supportive, not punishing—designed to help me stay on track, adapt as life changes, and move forward confidently without financial anxiety.
Scenario 1 · Student

First Time Student

Scenario Description

A student arriving at the Nelnet payment portal for the very first time. They authenticate via SAML SSO from their university's student portal — no separate Nelnet login is created. Upon authentication, a full ERP sync delivers all account data automatically.

This student has no existing payment methods, authorized parties, or communication preferences on file. Every decision in this flow is being made for the first time this term. The prototype for this scenario is the "First Time Student" prototype on the index page.

Institution Configuration — This Scenario
AuthenticationSAML SSO from university portal
ERP integrationFull sync — charges, aid, enrollment
Tuition insuranceEnabled (GradGuard)
Payment plan offers1 — Pay Over Time
Accepted payment methodsACH (bank) + Credit Card
University disclosures3 (individually acknowledged)
POT first payment windowMust be within 30 days of today
POT minimum down payment5% of plan balance
Authentication & Data Pre-population

The student arrives exclusively via SAML SSO from the university portal. No Nelnet-specific username or password entry occurs within Horizon.

ERP / SSO

Upon authentication, the system must automatically populate: student first name, last name, preferred name (if available), student ID, current term, total charges, applied financial aid, anticipated financial aid, balance due, primary email address, and phone number — all sourced from the ERP. None of these require manual entry.

Why: No duplicate entry. The student should never type what the institution already knows. This is a core product principle.

ERP / SSORequired
1
Balance Overview

Present the student's full account balance breakdown: total charges, applied financial aid, anticipated financial aid, and resulting balance due. All values are sourced from the ERP — no manual entry.

Why: Students must understand what they owe before making any payment decision. This is the anchor for all downstream choices in the flow.

ERP / SSO

The term "Anticipated" (adjacent to the anticipated aid figure) must be interactive — tapping or clicking it opens an inline explanation: anticipated aid has been subtracted from the balance shown, but is not yet officially applied to the student's school account. If it does not finalize, the student may still owe that amount.

Why: Anticipated aid is a leading driver of student confusion and support contacts. An inline explanation surfaces at the moment of need without adding a screen.

The balance breakdown must remain accessible to the student throughout the entire onboarding flow. They should be able to reference their balance at any point while making decisions.

2
Tuition Insurance Institution: GradGuard enabled

This step is shown only when the institution has tuition insurance (GradGuard) enabled. If not enabled, the flow proceeds directly to Step 3.

Institution settingIF: GradGuard enabled → show step

The insurance offer must not be presented until the correct premium can be calculated. Premium varies by the student's state of residence. Ask first: "Do you live in [institution state]?" — Yes / No. This is the only question shown initially.

Why: Showing an offer before knowing the applicable premium is either misleading or requires a rate percentage — which means nothing actionable to most consumers.

IF: Student answers "No, I live out of state"

Reveal a state selector so the student can indicate their state of residence. Once a state is selected, the correct premium is calculated and the offer can be shown.

Once state is resolved (either "Yes, I live in [state]" or an out-of-state selection is made), show the tuition insurance offer card. Display the premium as a dollar amount only — do not show a rate percentage.

Why: A percentage rate is not actionable. The dollar cost is what allows the student to make a real decision.

The student must have three response options: Accept, Decline, or Decide Later.

IF: Student accepts tuition insurance

Display an immediate confirmation note at the point of acceptance: "[Premium amount] will be added to your sign-up costs today." The student must know the premium is part of today's payment before they proceed.

The insurance premium must be included in every downstream total-due-today calculation: the Pay Over Time down payment breakdown (Step 3), the payment confirmation screen (Step 4), and the success screen. The total must never show just the down payment or enrollment fee in isolation.

Cascades to: Step 3 · Step 4 · Success screen
3
How to Pay

Present the available payment paths. Options are determined by institution settings. Always available: Pay in Full, Schedule a Payment. Conditional: Pay Over Time (shown only if the institution offers a payment plan).

Institution setting

Each option must describe what it means for the student — not just label the option. The Pay Over Time option description: "Split your balance into multiple payments over a longer period of time." Do not surface plan details (installment count, fees) at this selection step — those are determined in the inline setup that follows.

Why: Decision-first principle — explain the outcome, not the mechanics. Details are surfaced only when the student has chosen this path.

PATH: Pay in Full

Show the full balance due. Student proceeds to Step 4 (payment method collection) for the full amount.

PATH: Schedule a Payment

Allow the student to select a future payment date. Student then proceeds to Step 4 to enter their payment method for the scheduled amount.

PATH: Pay Over Time Institution: plan offered

The entire Pay Over Time setup must happen inline on the same screen where the student selected "Pay Over Time." The system must not navigate to a separate setup screen. The balance card must remain visible at the top of the screen throughout the setup.

Why: Keeping the balance context visible while making payment structure decisions reduces cognitive load and prevents the student from losing track of what they're paying.

Setup uses a progressive reveal pattern across 4 sequential decisions. Each decision step collapses to a summary row (with an Edit button) once confirmed. Editing any step resets all downstream confirmations — for example, editing frequency invalidates the installment count and down payment, since those depend on frequency.

Why: Downstream choices (installment count, last payment date) depend directly on upstream choices (frequency, first date). Preventing stale combinations avoids the student accidentally committing to an invalid plan.

Sub-step A — Payment Frequency. Options: Weekly, Every 2 weeks, Monthly. Once confirmed, collapses to a summary row showing the selected frequency, with an Edit button.

Sub-step B — First Payment Date. The institution's date constraint must be displayed prominently before the student selects a date: "Your first payment must be within 30 days of today." The date picker must enforce this: minimum = tomorrow, maximum = 30 days from today. Once confirmed, collapses to a summary row showing the selected date.

Why: Institution requirements must be communicated upfront — not as a validation error after the student picks an invalid date. This is a decision-framing requirement, not just a validation rule.

Institution setting: 30-day window

Sub-step C — Number of Installments. The options shown depend on the frequency chosen in Sub-step A:
 · Weekly: 4, 8, 13, or 26 installments
 · Every 2 weeks: 3, 6, 9, or 12 installments
 · Monthly: 3, 6, 9, or 12 installments
The collapsed summary row must show: "[N] installments — last payment [calculated date] — [frequency]." The last payment date is calculated from Sub-steps A + B + C.

Why: The last payment date is the most meaningful signal of commitment length. Surfacing it in the summary immediately after selection confirms the student understands when the plan ends.

Sub-step D — Down Payment. A slider allowing the student to choose how much to pay today as a down payment. Slider minimum = 5% of the plan balance (the institution-required minimum), clearly labeled as "School min (5%)." Slider maximum = full balance. As the slider moves, the per-installment payment amount updates dynamically.

Why: Giving students control over the down payment empowers cash flow management. The 5% floor is a school requirement and must be communicated proactively — not encountered as a blocking error.

Institution setting: 5% minimum

Throughout Sub-step D, display a live breakdown of the total due today — updated in real time as the slider moves. The breakdown must include:
 · Down payment (selected amount)
 · Nelnet enrollment fee
 · Tuition insurance premium (only if accepted in Step 2)
 · Total due today

Why: The student is deciding how much leaves their account today. They need to see the complete picture in real time — not just the down payment value in isolation.

IF: Insurance accepted → include premium in breakdown
4
Payment Method & Confirmation

Collect the payment method for the amount due today (full balance, scheduled amount, or Pay Over Time total). Accepted methods are determined by institution settings. This scenario: ACH (bank account) and Credit Card.

Institution setting: accepted methods

If a saved payment method exists from a previous term, pre-select and display it. Only request new payment details if no saved method is on file.

Why: No duplicate entry. Payment methods carry forward term over term.

IF: Saved method on file → pre-populate

Before charging, present a full confirmation screen showing a complete breakdown of everything due today. For Pay Over Time, this includes: down payment, Nelnet enrollment fee, tuition insurance premium (if applicable), total due today, plus the plan summary — installment count × installment amount, payment frequency, first payment date, and remaining balance after today's payment.

IF: Insurance accepted → show premium rowIF: POT path → show plan summary
5
Authorized Parties

Allow the student to designate authorized parties — individuals who can access and pay on the student's behalf. Authorized parties are managed within Nelnet's system, not by the institution. The student controls who has access.

Why: Many students have parents or guardians who manage payments. Capturing this during onboarding means the AP can be active immediately, without a separate re-entry loop later.

Collect name and email for each authorized party. Send each AP an invitation email with their account setup link immediately upon submission.

The student can add multiple authorized parties, or skip this step entirely.

IF: One or more authorized parties added

Post-onboarding: the Manage Authorized Parties quick action on the home screen must show the names of the APs added here, not just a generic label.

Cascades to: Home screen quick actions

Post-onboarding: if any authorized party subsequently sets up a Pay Over Time plan, a new "AP Pay Over Time Plans" quick action must appear on the student's home screen.

Cascades to: Home screen · IF AP creates POT plan

Communication Preferences

Transactional SMS (payment confirmations, reminders, account alerts) must be enabled and is not toggleable. Label it as required for account activity.

Why: Transactional messages are required to service the account. Opt-out is not available for this category.

Marketing/promotional SMS is opt-in and toggleable. The default for a first-time student is off unless the institution has configured a different default. Whatever state the student sets here carries forward to their Account Details screen.

Cascades to: Account Details · SMS opt-in state

The student's email address is pre-populated from SSO/ERP. No re-entry required.

Consumer Home Screen
Greeting & Identity Area

Greet the student by first name — large, left-aligned. Account access (initials avatar + "Account" label) is positioned on the right side of the greeting area and is always tappable to reach Account Details. The initials avatar is the only account access entry point; no duplicate link elsewhere in the header.

To-dos Section

A "To dos" section appears above the account summary. To-do items are entirely dynamic — generated from the student's current account state. Show nothing in this section if there are no actionable to-dos.

If an open (unpaid, unscheduled) balance exists: show a to-do card displaying the open amount, with the copy "Hasn't been paid or scheduled to be paid yet."

IF: Open balance exists

If a payment is due within the next 30 days: surface it as a to-do with the amount and due date.

IF: Payment due within 30 days

Account activity from the last 30 days must be surfaced in this area as context — payments made, plan changes, etc.

Account Summary Section

Show a visual balance bar below the "Account summary" section label. The bar is composed of up to two segments representing how the total balance is distributed:

 · On Pay Over Time plan (teal/green segment) — only present if student has an active POT plan
 · Open balance (accent color segment) — always present if there is any open/unpaid balance

The proportions of each segment reflect the actual split of the student's total balance.

IF: POT plan active → two-segment bar IF: No POT plan → single segment

Below the balance bar: display the open balance dollar amount prominently on the left. The label "Open balance" appears to the right of the number. Brief supporting text explains what "open balance" means in this context.

A "View full statement" button must be present within the account summary section — not in the quick actions. It is a secondary/outline-style button (not a primary CTA).

Why: Statement access is contextually adjacent to the balance — that's where students will look for it. It does not belong in quick actions as a distinct action.

Quick Actions (Dynamic, Priority Ordered)

Quick actions are shown and ordered dynamically based on the student's account state. The full ordered list:

1. My Pay Over Time Plan — shown only if the student has an active POT plan
2. Manage Authorized Parties — always shown; sub-label: "People with full payment access to your account"
3. Guest Payment Link — always shown; framed as sending a one-time payment link
4. Make a Payment — always shown; sub-label sets expectation: make a payment now
5. Account History — always shown
6. AP Pay Over Time Plans — shown only if at least one authorized party has an active POT plan

IF: Student has POT → show #1 IF: Any AP has POT → show #6

Account Details

Accessible from the initials avatar on the home screen. Must display: first name, last name, preferred name (editable), student ID (read-only — from ERP). Manage Authorized Parties

Show all authorized parties with their current active/inactive status. Student can toggle any AP between active and inactive. Deactivating an AP must require an explicit confirmation step to prevent accidental removal. Student can invite additional APs directly from this screen.

Why: The student owns access control. A confirmation step on deactivation protects against accidentally cutting off someone who is actively managing payments.

Guest Payment Link

Frame this as a one-time payment link — not account access. Show the student's current open balance as context. Allow the student to set a cap amount (must be ≤ open balance) and an optional note to the guest. Explicitly and clearly distinguish this from an authorized party: the guest has zero ongoing account access — they can only pay once via this link.

Why: Some students want a family member to make a single payment without granting ongoing account visibility or control. The distinction between "guest payer" and "authorized party" must be unambiguous — otherwise students may unknowingly grant more access than intended, or be confused when the guest can't pay again.

AP Pay Over Time Plans

Read-only view of Pay Over Time plans set up by the student's authorized parties. Accessible from quick action #6 on the home screen, which is only shown when at least one AP has an active POT plan.

Onboarding Features

How to read this section

Each feature below represents a discrete step or capability within the onboarding flow. Requirements here are written cross-scenario — they describe behavior across all current and future scenarios. Scenario chips indicate which scenarios each feature currently applies to, and which are pending.

When a new scenario is added, return to each relevant feature and update its requirements and scenario chips to reflect any scenario-specific business rules.

Authentication
How consumers enter the Horizon experience
S1 · First Time Student S2 · Returning Student S3–S6 · Authorized Parties
Cross-scenario rule: Authentication method differs by user type. Students always authenticate via institution SSO. Authorized parties authenticate via a Nelnet-issued invite link and create independent credentials — they never use the student's login.

Student authentication is exclusively via institution SAML SSO. Students access Horizon by clicking a link within their institution's portal. The session is pre-authenticated — no Nelnet username or password is required. Students never create a Horizon-specific login.

Why: Eliminates credential friction and ensures identity is always validated through the institution's existing auth system.

ERP: identity + enrollment passed via SSO token

The SSO token carries enrollment, identity, and authorization context. The institution passes student name, email, enrollment status, and account identifiers as part of the SAML assertion. Horizon reads these values directly — students are never asked to re-enter what the school already knows.

RequiredERP integration

Authorized party (AP) authentication is separate from the student's credentials. When a student invites an authorized party, Nelnet sends that person a unique invite link. The AP follows the link to create their own Nelnet account. They never share or reuse the student's login. Their access and permissions are scoped to exactly what the student granted.

IF: Student has set up APs → AP invite link exists

Session state persists through onboarding. If a student begins onboarding but doesn't complete it in one session (e.g., chooses "I'll complete this later" on the authorized parties step), their progress is saved. When they re-enter via SSO, they resume from where they left off — or can choose to start over.

Balance Overview
How account charges and financial aid are presented
S1 · First Time Student S2 · Returning Student S3–S4 · New AP (Payer)
Cross-scenario rule: Balance is always derived from ERP in real time. No balance is ever hard-coded or estimated by Horizon. Returning students see a comparison of prior-term vs. current-term amounts. APs see only the balance components the student has granted visibility to.

Display a full balance breakdown on entry to the payment decision step. Show: total charges (tuition + institutional fees), financial aid applied, school enrollment fee (if applicable), and the resulting net balance. Each component must be labeled clearly — no abbreviations the student may not recognize.

ERP: charges + financial aidInstitution: enrollment fee amount

Distinguish estimated vs. finalized balances. If the ERP has not yet posted final charges (e.g., registration is still open), the balance is marked "estimated" with a plain-language explanation. Decisions made on an estimated balance (e.g., POT enrollment) are confirmed again once the balance is finalized.

ERP: balance finalization flag

Balance math must always resolve to $0 open balance when fully enrolled in Pay Over Time. net balance − down payment − on-plan amount = $0. No rounding or UI artifact should show a non-zero open balance when the student has enrolled their entire balance in a plan.

Required · Bug prevention

Insurance premium is excluded from the "balance owed" calculation. When a student accepts tuition insurance, the premium is charged immediately as a separate transaction — it does not add to the open balance that feeds the payment plan or pay-in-full flow.

IF: GradGuard enabled + student accepted insurance

Tuition Insurance
GradGuard integration — opt-in/opt-out decision during onboarding
S1 · First Time Student S2 · Returning Student Institution-configured
Cross-scenario rule: Tuition insurance is an institution-level toggle. When enabled, every new student in onboarding is presented the offer. The offer is not re-surfaced to returning students who already made a decision in a prior term unless the institution resets the offer. APs are never offered tuition insurance — it is a student-only decision.

Insurance step is conditional on institution enabling GradGuard. If GradGuard is not enabled for the institution, this step is skipped entirely — no placeholder, no mention of it.

Institution: GradGuard toggleIF: GradGuard enabled → show insurance step

Present the insurance offer with the calculated premium for this student's enrollment. The premium is derived from the student's total charges and the institution's GradGuard plan. Show the annual premium amount, what is covered (medical withdrawal, academic dismissal, etc.), and the GradGuard plan name. Do not require the student to navigate elsewhere to understand what they're buying.

ERP: enrollment charges for premium calc

Student may accept or decline. Both paths must be equally accessible — no dark patterns. "Accept" and "Decline" must be visually equivalent choices. Do not pre-select "Accept", do not style "Decline" as a secondary/tertiary action.

Required

If accepted: premium is charged immediately as a separate transaction. The insurance premium appears in Recent Activity on the home screen — NOT in Upcoming Payments. Required

Features · Home Screen

Home Screen Features

Context

The home screen is the persistent hub a consumer returns to after completing onboarding. It surfaces the most important information and actions for their current account state — adapting in real time as charges post, plans change, payments are made, and authorized parties take action.

Requirements below define each home screen module independently. As more scenarios are added (APs, returning students), return here to extend each module's rules for those user types.

⚙️
Account Settings
Personal info, payment methods, authorized parties, and notification preferences
S1 · First Time Student S2 · Returning Student S3–S6 · Authorized Parties
Cross-scenario rule: Settings belong to the individual user. A student's settings page always shows authorized party management. An AP's settings page never includes AP management (APs cannot invite sub-parties). Returning students see pre-populated values from prior term.

Account Settings is accessible from the home screen at all times — not gated behind completion of any other action. A persistent settings entry point (gear icon or equivalent) must be available in the home screen header or navigation area.

Required

Settings contains four sub-sections: (1) Personal contact info — name, email addresses, phone numbers; (2) Payment methods — saved bank accounts and cards, with add/remove; (3) Authorized parties — view current APs, add new, remove, or update permissions; (4) Notification preferences — same informational/marketing SMS split as during onboarding.

Required

Changes made in Settings take effect immediately and do not trigger re-onboarding. Updating a phone number, adding a payment method, or removing an AP does not restart or re-surface any previously completed onboarding steps.

Required

An AP can be removed by the student at any time from Settings. Removing an AP immediately revokes their access — they can no longer log in or view account information. An email notification is sent to the AP at the time of removal.

Required
✅
To-dos
Outstanding actions that require the student's attention
S1 · First Time Student S2 · Returning Student S3–S6 · Authorized Parties
Cross-scenario rule: To-do items are scoped to the user's role. A student sees to-dos related to their account decisions and payments. An AP sees to-dos relevant to their specific responsibilities (e.g., a pending payment they were invited to make). To-dos for one user are never shown to another.

To-dos are action-required items that the user has not yet completed. Examples: unsigned disclosures, incomplete onboarding (deferred AP setup), payment due within 7 days, payment method expiring, open balance with no payment path set, AP invitation pending acceptance.

Required

Each to-do links directly to the relevant action. Tapping a to-do item routes the user to the exact screen or step where they can complete the action — not to a generic settings page.

Required

To-dos are automatically dismissed when the action is completed. No manual "dismiss" is needed for completed items — they disappear from the list. Time-sensitive items (payment past-due, method expired) remain until resolved even if previously acknowledged.

Required

When no to-dos exist, the section is either hidden or shows a neutral empty state. Do not show an empty to-do section with placeholder text that implies the user might be missing something. Empty state copy should be affirmative: e.g., "You're all caught up."

Required
🕐
Recent Activity
All account transactions from the last 30 days
S1 · First Time Student S2 · Returning Student S3–S4 · New AP (Payer)
Cross-scenario rule: Returning students' recent activity spans the current and prior term if within 30 days. APs only see activity they themselves initiated, unless the student has granted full account visibility. The 30-day window is the same for all user types.

Recent Activity shows all account transactions posted in the last 30 days. Transaction types include: payments made (by student or AP), credits applied, financial aid posted, institutional fees charged, tuition insurance premium charged, plan enrollment fees.

RequiredERP: transaction feed

Tuition insurance premium appears in Recent Activity — not in Upcoming Payments. Because the premium is charged at the moment of acceptance (not on a future schedule), it is a completed transaction. Showing it in Upcoming would incorrectly imply it's a future obligation.

IF: GradGuard enabled + student accepted → premium in recent activity

Each activity item shows: description, date, amount, and status. Status values: Posted, Pending, Reversed. Amount is signed: payments are shown as negative (reducing balance), charges are positive.

Required

Items are sorted chronologically, most recent first. If there are more than a practical display limit (e.g., 5 items), a "View all activity" link reveals the full list or navigates to a dedicated transaction history screen.

Required

Upcoming Payments
Scheduled payments due in the next 30 days
S1 · First Time Student S2 · Returning Student S3–S4 · New AP (Payer)
Cross-scenario rule: APs who have their own payment plan see their own upcoming installments — not the student's full schedule (unless explicitly granted). The 30-day window is standard across user types.

Upcoming Payments shows only scheduled installments due within the next 30 days. This section does not include: one-time payments the user may choose to make, the tuition insurance premium (already charged), or charges not yet due. RequiredIF: payment path = POT → upcoming installments shown

Account Presentment
Current balance snapshot, plan status, and enrollment context
S1 · First Time Student S2 · Returning Student S3–S4 · New AP (Payer)
Cross-scenario rule: APs see only the balance amounts the student has granted visibility to — if the student set a 50% cap, the AP sees 50% of the actual balance. Returning students see current-term balance, with prior-term context available on request. Balance is always synced from ERP in real time — never cached or stale.

Account Presentment is the primary balance summary on the home screen. It shows: total charges for the current term, financial aid applied, net balance, current open balance (amount not covered by a payment plan or prior payments), and amount currently on a payment plan.

RequiredERP: real-time balance

Balance components are shown as a clear breakdown — not a single number. Students should be able to understand at a glance what they owe, what's covered by aid, and what remains to be paid vs. what's already on a plan. A single "Balance: $X,XXX" without context fails this requirement.

Required

IF: payment path = POT → plan summary visible on home screen

Open balance is $0 when the student is fully enrolled in a POT plan. The math must always resolve: net balance − down payment − on-plan amount = $0 open balance. No rounding artifact should produce a non-zero open balance when the student has enrolled their entire net balance in a plan.

Institution Configuration

Institution Configuration Options

Scope of this section

All scenarios represent students authenticating via SAML SSO directly from their university's portal, with full ERP integration — charges, financial aid, and enrollment data synced automatically. Authorized payers are managed within Nelnet's system; the student controls who has access and at what level.

This section documents every institution-level setting that changes what a student or authorized party sees and can do inside the Horizon experience. Settings are configured per institution at the time of implementation. Where the current prototype uses a specific value, that value is marked Prototype default.

💳
Payment Methods
ACH / Bank Account
Allow students and authorized payers to pay via direct bank transfer (ACH). No service fee applies to ACH payments.
Options Enabled Prototype default Disabled
Consumer impact: If disabled, bank account will not appear as a payment option. Students will only see card-based methods — meaning the 2.75% service fee will always apply.
Credit Card
Allow payments via credit card. A service fee applies; the percentage is institution-configured (see below).
Options Enabled Prototype default Disabled
Consumer impact: If disabled, credit card will not appear as a payment option. The service fee disclosure note is also suppressed when all card types are disabled.
Debit Card
Allow payments via debit card. Treated the same as credit card for fee and routing purposes unless the institution explicitly separates them.
Options Enabled Prototype default Disabled
Consumer impact: Same as credit card. If both credit and debit are disabled but ACH is on, students pay via bank account only with no service fee.
Card Service Fee Percentage
The percentage added to any card-based payment (credit or debit). Disclosed to the student before they select a payment method, and reflected in the payment confirmation total.
Options Institution-configured % 2.75% in prototype
Consumer impact: The fee notice reads "Card payments include a [X]% service fee" — the percentage is dynamic. A student paying $1,000 by card at 2.75% sees their total displayed as $1,027.50 before confirming. ACH always shows no fee.

A one-time, non-refundable fee charged by the institution at the time the student confirms their payment setup. Appears as a separate line item in the balance breakdown.
Options $0 (waived) or institution-set amount $25 in prototype
Consumer impact: When set to $0 the enrollment fee line is hidden entirely. When set to a positive amount it appears in the balance summary and is included in the total the student pays at confirmation. It does not roll into POT installments.

Master on/off for payment plan offerings. When disabled, students only see the Pay in Full path during onboarding.
Options Enabled Prototype default Disabled
Consumer impact: If disabled, the "How to Pay" step shows only Pay in Full. The POT plan setup screens, plan confirmation, and the "Set Up Payment Plan" quick action on the home screen are all suppressed.

How many distinct Pay Over Time plans the institution makes available to students. Each plan can have different down payment requirements, installment counts, dates, and fees.
Options 1 plan Prototype default 2 plans 3+ plans
Consumer impact: When exactly 1 plan is configured, the student skips the plan-selection screen and goes directly to down payment selection for that plan. When 2 or more plans exist, the student sees a plan picker first and chooses between the available options.
Down Payment Configuration (per plan)
Defines how the required down payment is determined for each plan. Can be a fixed percentage of the net balance, a tiered range the student selects within, or a flat minimum amount.
Options Fixed % of balance Prototype default Student-selected % within a min–max range Flat minimum dollar amount
Consumer impact: A fixed percentage requires no input from the student — the down payment is shown and confirmed. A range lets the student choose how much to put down (within bounds), showing how their selection changes the installment amount in real time. This is one of the highest-impact configuration decisions for perceived flexibility.
Number of Installments (per plan)
How many equal installment payments the remaining balance is divided into after the down payment. Drives the per-installment amount displayed during plan confirmation.
Options Institution-configured count per plan 4 in prototype
Consumer impact: Directly determines the installment amount shown in the natural-language plan summary: "you'll be charged [frequency] — [N] payments of [$X] each." Smaller installment counts mean larger per-payment amounts.

How often installment payments are charged — monthly is the most common, but institutions may offer bi-weekly or term-specific schedules.
Options Monthly Prototype default Bi-weekly Custom schedule
Consumer impact: The payment frequency label appears in the plan summary ("charged monthly") and determines the spacing between dates in the installment schedule. It also affects the Upcoming Payments module — a monthly plan will show at most one upcoming installment at a time.
Plan Start Date & End Date (per plan)
The date range during which the plan is active. The first installment date is derived from the plan start date; the final installment aligns with the plan end date.
Options Institution-configured per plan and term End date: Dec 15, 2026 in prototype
Consumer impact: Both dates appear in the plan confirmation summary. If today's date is after the plan start, the first installment due date is adjusted to the next valid payment date in the schedule.
Plan Enrollment Fee (per plan)
An optional fee charged to the student when they enroll in a specific Pay Over Time plan. Distinct from the school enrollment fee — this is plan-specific and may differ across plans at the same institution.
Options $0 (no fee) Institution-set flat amount
Consumer impact: When a plan enrollment fee exists, it is shown as a separate line in the plan confirmation screen and added to the amount due today alongside the down payment. It is not rolled into future installments.
